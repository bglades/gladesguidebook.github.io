5 Colorado Alpine Hikes

Jump to each hike:

  1. Cupid and Grizzly Peak
  2. Sniktau Mountain
  3. Square Top Mountain
  4. Lake Isabelle
  5. St. Mary's Glacier to James Peak

1. Cupid and Grizzly Peak

Trailhead: Loveland Pass Summit
Parking: Park at Loveland Pass Summit
Distance: 6.7mi
Elevation Gain: 2,631ft
Cupid Peak Summit: 13,117ft
Grizzly Peak Summit: 13,334ft
Difficulty: Hard

The trail begins on the east side of the road. There are trails on both sides of the road so be aware.
At the first summit the trail splits. Take the right fork. From here the trail leads to Cupid Peak. You will
easily see Grizzly Peak ahead of you. Grizzly Peak is a harder climb but worth it. You will have amazing
views of Grey's and Torrey's Peaks, Mt. Sniktau, and Summit County.


2. Sniktau Mountain

Trailhead: Loveland Pass Summit
Parking:Park at Loveland Pass Summit
Distance: 4mi
Elevation Gain: 1,587ft
Summit: 13,240ft
Difficulty: Moderately Hard

The parking and trailhead are the same as above. At the fork you will go left instead.
This will take you directly to Sniktau Mountain. Be aware of the false summit!
You really only need about 3-4 hours to do this trail. It's a quick and rewarding peak.


3. Square Top Mountain

Trailhead/Parking: There are two parking areas at the summit of Guanella Pass. The trail starts
at the parking lot on the west side of the road. It is often difficult to find
parking in the east lot as this lot is the trailhead to Mt. Bierstadt.
Distance: 6.5mi
Elevation Gain: 2,400ft
Summit: 13,749ft
Difficutly: Hard


This peak is harder than many of the Colorado 14ers. In fact, Mt. Bierstadt is just across
the street. Hundreds of people will be hiking Mt. Bierstadt but few will be
on Square Top Mountain. You will get the same views without all the people.
Plus parking in the west lot is a bit smoother. Keep an eye out for signs and kairns.
The trail forks for Square Top Lakes. This hike is worth doing
if you'd like an easier day. If you're headed for the summit, stay left at the fork
headed away from the lakes. The trail takes you to the southwest side
of the mountain for the upward trudge. The final climb up is a scramble with
loose gravel. Stay away from the north side as there are large cliffs.
Please be aware that there are multiple false summits. Keep going. It is worth the trudge.


4. Lake Isabelle

Trailhead: Isabelle Glacier Trail #908. Southeast side of Brainard Lake.

Parking: Park at Brainard Lake. There is a fee to enter the Brainard Lake Recreation Area.

Distance: 4.2mi

Elevation Gain: about 500ft

Lake Elevation: ~10,900ft

Difficulty: Moderate

This hike starts near the Peak to Peak Highway. You'll have a beautiful drive
to Brainard Lake Recreation Area but you'll need to pay the fee to enter
the area. You are also able to park at the Gateway Trailhead
and hike to Brainard Lake. Sometimes there is no parking within the recreation area
and you'll have to do this. This will add
4 miles onto your round trip hike. You can easily
avoid this by showing up early(before 8am). The trail starts on
the southest edge of the lake. There are signs and navigating
here is easy. The trail is also easy to follow. There are
no major forks to watch for. There are a few smaller lakes
before you make it to Isabelle Glacier. There are plenty
of moose in this area so keep an eye out. Once you make it to the
lake the trail continues to Isabelle Glacier. If you're looking for
a longer and more difficult day, consider hiking to the glacier.

5. St. Mary's Glacier to James Peak

Trailhead: St.Mary's Trailhead
Parking: St. Mary's Trailhead. Parking is $5.
Distance: 8mi
Elevation Gain: 2,950ft
Summit: 13,294ft
Difficulty Hard

Start out at the St. Mary's Trailhead. Hike to St. Mary's Lake and make
your way to the Glacier just above the lake. The trail
is obvious and there are signs. Hike to the top of the glacier
and follow the trail to the top of the pass. There are plenty
of forks but you'll want to keep going straight. Don't take
any fork. Again, there are signs and kairns.
Making your way up James Peak is the hardest part. For an
easier day, turn around at St. Mary's Lake or Glacier.
